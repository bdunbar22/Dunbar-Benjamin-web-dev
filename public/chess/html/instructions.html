Let's learn how to play!

Pieces and Moves
Symbol Piece Ability
Pawn Can move one block forward. Can capture one block diagonally forward.
Rook Can move freely along a row or a column. Can capture at destination.
Knight Moves one space normally and then one space diagonally in any direction. Can "jump" over other pieces. Can capture upon landing.
Bishop Can move freely diagonally. Can capture at destination.
Queen Can move freely along a row, column, or diagonal path. Can capture at destination.
King Can move to any of the 8 adjacent squares. Can capture at destination. Can not move into "check" or "checkmate".

Exceptions
Piece Special Case
Pawn When a pawn is first moved it can go two spaces forward.
Pawn En Passant: If an opposing pawn moved two spaces forward on its last move. And is now next to a pawn of the opposing player, the current player can move diagonally into the space that the opposing pawn skipped over and still capture the opposing pawn.
Pawn Promotion: Upon reaching the other end of the board the pawn can be promoted to any piece of the same color except to a King. Extra Pieces are povided on the sides for promotion if needed.
King, Rook Castling: If all of the spaces between a King and a Rook are vacant, neither the King nor the Rook in question have made a move yet during the game, and movement through any of the open squares would not be the King in danger by being in "check", then the king may move two spaces towards the rook and the rook will move to the square immediately inside of the kings position. Initiate by moving King.
King Check: If the King is in a position such that the opposing player could capture the King on their next turn then it is in check. If a players King is in check they MUST make a move such that the King is no longer in check at the end of their turn. Note: You must watch for this. Not watched by computer.
King Checkmate: If one of the players King is in check and the player can not make a move such that their King is no longer in check, then a checkmate has been reached and the other player wins. In this version, the opposing player must now capture the King to see the win message.
